How we met

On a breezy fall afternoon, Freshta was minding her own business eating her lunch. It just so happened that she was one of the few people that got a free falafel sandwich from a local falafel restaurant. As she was making her way through her falafels, a strange man (Aaron) and his friend walked in. As he noticed her sandwich, he exclaimed. All he felt was pure terror. How could he, the person who always knew where the free food was, forget to go claim his free falafel sandwich? At this point, Freshta was looking up at him with concern. Aaron explained that he and his friend had planned on going to get falafels that day too.

A few minutes passed and Freshta could feel that she was being watched. Little did she know that it was actually her falafels that were being watched. Because she was taught to always share, she begrudgingly asked Aaron if he would like a taste of her sandwich. Of course, he could never say no to some free food. He brought over a fork and a frisbee to collect the delicacy. You might be wondering why a grown man eats his food off a frisbee. Freshta wondered that too and she still doesn’t know why.

As Freshta walked out after an odd lunch to say the least, Aaron did one more thing that made her turn back. He said “Khuda Hafiz”, which is how you say goodbye in Farsi, the language Freshta’s family speaks. She was intrigued and confused about how he learned these words, but he wouldn’t give away his secret. Freshta knew that he was a little weird, but he was special. What she didn’t know was that one day, she would spend the rest of her life with him.

29th May

Henna Night and Wedding Reception Start Time: 1 PM

To help decrease the exposure to COVID-19, we are combining the Henna Night and Wedding Reception (previously two separate events).

Henna Night traditions: The Bride and Groom will start off the night wearing traditional Afghan clothes. As part of the tradition, seven girls will place henna on the bride’s hand. Afterwards, other guests are welcome to do creative henna designs on their own hands or each other’s.

Wedding Reception traditions: The Bride and Groom re-enter the room and walk down the aisle together. After the bride and groom reach the stage, there will then be a series of traditions, including a gift exchange and the mirror ceremony (bride and groom are covered with a shawl and look at each other in a mirror in order to imagine their life together).
